Dr Matthew Banks

BSc MB MRCP PhD

University College London Hospitals NHS Trust

Dr Banks is a Consultant Gastroenterologist and honorary senior lecturer at University College London Hospitals NHS Trust, where he is also Director of Endoscopy.


During his medical studies at UCL he was given a scholarship to undertake a BSc in tropical medicine. He gained his core gastrointestinal training at St Bartholemew’s and The London Hospital and has undertaken endoscopic fellowships in Australia and Japan. His PhD investigated mechanisms of diarrhoea, a subject on which he has written many articles and chapters.


His clinical interests and expertise now focus on advanced endoscopic techniques, particularly for the detection and management of early gastrointestinal cancer including polypectomy. He still maintains a keen interest in general gastroenterology including complex functional disorders.


His current research includes diagnosis and management of Barrett’s neoplasia and sedation in endoscopy.
