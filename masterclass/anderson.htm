Dr Simon Anderson

MRCP MD

Guy’s & St Thomas’ NHS Foundation Trust

Dr Anderson is a Consultant Physician and Gastroenterologist at Guy’s and St Thomas’ NHS Foundation Trust. He also leads an acute general medicine team at St Thomas’ Hospital. Away from professional practice, Dr Anderson puts his in-depth knowledge to further good use as a head tutor for undergraduates at King’s College London University. He is also a regular teacher and clinical examiner of their postgraduate programme.


As one of the foremost practitioners in his field, Dr Anderson set up and leads the wireless capsule endoscopy service and balloon enteroscopy service at Guy’s and St Thomas’ NHS Foundation Trust. His interests include all therapeutic endoscopy and colonoscopy, inflammatory bowel disease (ulcerative colitis and Crohn’s disease,) wireless capsule endoscopy, balloon enteroscopy and general liver disease.


Further interests include research into techniques for the diagnosis and treatment of small-intestinal disease and ulcerative colitis. He is also breaking new ground as lead investigator in a trial of a new treatment for Crohn’s disease, and is a co-investigator into the genetics of inflammatory bowel disease.
